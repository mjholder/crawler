## What I worked on

Refactored GUI/game signal wiring to enforce clean separation. Previously `game.gd` reached into GUI child nodes to connect button signals directly (`$GUI/MainMenu/StartButton.pressed`, `$GUI/PauseMenu/QuitToMainButton.pressed`). Now GUI handles all its own button connections internally and exposes semantic signals that game.gd connects to.

Key changes:

- **`scripts/gui.gd`** — Added three signals (`start_requested`, `quit_to_main_requested`, `attack_requested`). Moved StartButton and QuitToMainButton `.pressed` connections into GUI's own `_ready()`. Implemented `_on_start_button_pressed()`, `_on_quit_to_main_button_pressed()`, and `_on_attack_button_pressed()` to emit the new signals.
- **`scripts/game.gd`** — Removed direct `$GUI/MainMenu/StartButton` and `$GUI/PauseMenu/QuitToMainButton` signal connections. Now connects to `_gui.start_requested`, `_gui.quit_to_main_requested`, `_gui.attack_requested` in `_ready()`. Renamed handler functions to public-API-style names: `_on_start_button_pressed()` → `start_game()`, `_on_quit_to_main()` → `quit_to_main()`. `attack_action()` unchanged. This also wired up the AttackButton → `attack_action()` flow which wasn't connected before.
